Daniel Ricciardo Accident: How Did He Break His Wrist? Injuries Details

A noteworthy occurrence occurred during the second free practice session ahead of the Dutch Grand Prix this weekend, capturing the interest of Formula 1 fans all around the globe. Oscar Piastri’s McLaren crashed into the barriers as the vehicles raced around Zandvoort’s difficult track. What happened next, though, caught the crowd off guard. Daniel Ricciardo hit badly behind Piastri only seconds later, resulting in a major injury. The burning question today is, “How did Daniel Ricciardo break his wrist?”

This event not only forced Ricciardo to withdraw from the Dutch Grand Prix, but it also created an unexpected chance for New Zealander Liam Lawson to make his Formula One debut with AlphaTauri.

The First Crash

It all started when Oscar Piastri lost control of his McLaren and crashed into Zandvoort’s barriers. This track bend proved difficult for drivers, particularly during high-pressure practice sessions.

Unfortunate Collision of Ricciardo

Ricciardo, who was closely behind Piastri, was involved in a serious collision minutes later. The impact of the crash caused Ricciardo to receive a wrist injury, which sparked instant worry among the F1 world.

Reactions in a Hurry

Ricciardo was clearly in tremendous discomfort, as seen by his instant radio shout. His statements conveyed the gravity of the injuries as well as the shock of the unanticipated collision.

Aftermath of the Dutch Grand Prix

With Ricciardo’s wrist injury confirmed, he was forced to withdraw from the Dutch Grand Prix. This mishap allowed New Zealander Liam Lawson to step in and make his Formula One debut with AlphaTauri.

AlphaTauri Takes an Unexpected Turn

With Ricciardo ruled out of the race, the team was forced to find a substitute. Liam Lawson, a rising star, was given the opportunity to perform on the great stage.

The Return of Daniel Ricciardo to Formula One

It’s worth noting that this event signaled a brief halt in Ricciardo’s comeback to Formula 1. Fans all across the world are rooting for his quick recovery and impatiently anticipating his return.
